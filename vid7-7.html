What will you improve/innovate/upgrade from the system developed?

In the film "Saving the World One Algorithm at a Time," we see incredible ideas that use AI to tackle pressing global issues. But these systems may always be made better and more innovative to increase their efficiency and impact. GPS collars and satellite tracking are extremely useful for keeping tabs on and preserving elephants in the context of the Mara Elephant Project. Real-time data analytics and predictive modeling could be useful additions to this system. Artificial intelligence algorithms might analyze data on elephant migration patterns, environmental factors, and poaching concerns to identify possible hotspots and inform rangers in advance. Aerial drones fitted with artificial intelligence (AI) cameras could improve coverage and response times for security issues. More efficient cameras and communication systems would be a welcome improvement to the TrailGuard anti-poaching system. With these upgrades, batteries would last longer and less repairs would be required in outlying places. A more effective conservation tool might be created by enhancing the system to identify other unlawful animal activities, such as habitat degradation.

Constant improvement of the "Guiseppe" algorithm is essential for its goal of creating plant-based alternatives to animal-based diets. To increase their popularity, innovations could enhance these plant-based alternatives' flavor, texture, and nutritional value. In addition, the larger environmental aims of reducing meat consumption would be in line with an emphasis on sustainability in ingredient selection and manufacturing practices.
Expanding the sensor network and increasing the speed at which data can be transmitted are two examples of potential improvements to earthquake early warning systems like Shake Alert. Machine learning enhancements could improve earthquake detection and prediction, leading to more accurate early alerts.
Finally, Descartes Lab's predictive modeling and picture analysis may benefit from a more comprehensive view of possible crises if it integrated more diverse data sources like social media trends and climatic data. Decision-makers could respond more appropriately to alerts if AI models were more interpretable and explicable.
To sum up, the technologies demonstrated in the video are remarkable, but constant innovation is required to keep up with the various threats. With these improvements implemented, we can use AI to its fullest extent, improving the world's health and sustainability in a way that lasts.
